Fridays for Lent: Roasted Veggie Bowl

Recipe courtesy of the Food Network

Active:
  30 min
Yield: 
2 servings

Get out one sheet pan, put all of the veggies and chickpeas on to the pan to bake. In the mean time make some quinoa and cut some avocado slices to finish off the dish.

My Recommendations:

  • Don't Like Spice? Forgo adding the curry powderspice onto the chickpeas. Instead I recommend using chili powder, not as spicy but still gives the dish some flavor.
  • Can't Find Quinoa? Substitute the quinoa with rice.
  • Supermarket Shortcut: Buy Premade Guacamole as a time saver.
  • To make dairy free: Do not use Greek yogurt. Instead I suggest just picking up some Hummus and having that on the side.

Ingredients

  • One 14-ounce can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 3/4 teaspoon spicy curry powder
  • 4 teaspoons olive oil
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 small red onion (about 4 ounces), cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 small sweet potato (about 7 ounces), peeled and chopped into 1/2-inch pieces
  • 4 ounces medium cremini mushrooms, halved
  • 1/3 cup fat-free Greek yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon tahini 
  • 1 lemon
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1/2 avocado, thinly sliced 

Directions

  1. Toss the chickpeas with the curry powder, 1 teaspoon of the oil, 1/4 teaspoon salt and a few grinds of black pepper in a medium bowl. Place on one side of a baking sheet.
  2. Arrange the onions next to the chickpeas, then the sweet potatoes and lastly the mushrooms.
  3. Toss the onions, sweet potatoes and mushrooms with the remaining 3 teaspoons oil, 1/4 teaspoon salt and a generous amount of black pepper.
  4. Bake until the chickpeas are a bit crunchy and the sweet potatoes are softened and starting to turn brown in a few spots, 25 to 30 minutes. 
  5. Meanwhile, whisk the yogurt, tahini and juice of half the lemon in a small bowl. Slice the remaining lemon into wedges. 
  6. Divide the quinoa between two bowls. Arrange half the vegetables and avocado slices on top of the quinoa, leaving a space for the yogurt in the middle. Dollop the yogurt in the empty space and serve with the lemon wedges.

St. Patricks Day Vegan Pesto Pasta

St. Patricks Day has come and gone but this years St. Patrick's Day was celebrated on a Sunday. Normally, for many families including mine, Sundays mean Pasta! St. Patricks Day normally shows people sporting green socks and eating Lucky Charms. But, there is more than just Corned Beef and Cabbage that you can have on this most famous of Catholic feast days.
So how about putting a green spin on your Sunday pasta meal? How should we do that? Well, the perfect place to start is by adding herbs and creating a pesto sauce.

Courtesy of: Dadda eats

Normally, pesto contains dairy ingredients, but this recipe courtesy of Samah of Dadaeats, it is Dairy Free!

 Prep Time
5 minutes
Total Time
5 minutes
Servings
4 Servings
Author
Samah Dada

Ingredients

  • 1 box pasta of choice
  • 3/4 bag of spinach
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • 4 TBSP extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1/4 cup of water
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • Small handful basil leaves or basil flakes to taste
  • Chili flakes to taste
  • Oregano to taste
  • Salt to taste
  • Pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Boil a full box of your pasta of choice
  2. Prep the pesto in a food processor/blender and pulse until mixed. Add seasonings to taste as you wish.
  3. When the pasta is boiled, drain and add all of the sauce to the pot.
  4. Mix thoroughly.
  5. Optional: Blanch grape tomatoes in chili infused olive oil to add some color and sweetness to the dish. And maybe some almond milk ricotta if you're into that, or even some pine nuts. This is great served hot or as a cold pasta salad!
